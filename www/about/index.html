What Is Gittip?

Giving
Gittip lets you give small weekly cash gifts to people you love and are inspired by, and people who make things that you use.
Recurring
Gifts are weekly. The intention is for people to depend on money received through Gittip in order to pay their bills, and bills are recurring.
Unrestricted
Gifts come with no strings attached. You don't know exactly where your gifts come from, and the maximum gift from one person to another is $100 per week.
Public
The total amount you give and the total amount you receive is public. Participants on both sides of the equation are rewarded publicly for their participation. (You can opt out of publicly displaying your total giving.)

Get Involved

We're building Gittip openly and in public, and you're welcome to join us. Here is how to get involved:

  • Newsletter. The best way to get involved is to subscribe to our newsletter. This goes out about once a week and summarizes all of the activity going on in the Gittip community. From there you'll be able to identify projects that are a good fit for you and get involved further. You can view past newsletters in this archive.
  • GitHub (different than Gittip!) is a collaboration platform used by the software teams within Gittip. Anyone can register an account there and participate in discussions on any issues related to the software side of Gittip.
  • Freenode is an internet relay chat (IRC) network. It's for online chatrooms. We hang out in the #gittip channel. You can join in using your web browser, and there's a read-only archive.

We also use Skype and Google Hangouts to talk by voice and/or video. Our Freenode chatroom is the launching place for these conversations.
